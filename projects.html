FMF ISSUE 01

the museum as a cathedral

A cathedral rises up above the skyline. Tries to reach for the skies. Acts as a signal.

It is precisely this signal function that gives it an unparalleled importance in the city. The role of the cathedral is, by its physical presence, to symbolise both the glory of god and of the church.

This once important physical presence has been diminished as the other buildings have significantly increased in size. Still, the essence of a place that attracts devotees both from the surrounding area and often from far away, has remained.

However, it is no longer really the cathedral that attracts people these days, but rather places of art. This may seem an odd, even misplaced assertion because for a long time no one imagined an alternative. And certainly not the museum as the alternative.
